City Guide

Overview

City Guide is a search-and-discovery web application. The app guides users through a city, shows them places around and provides personalized recommendations based on users’ previous browsing history.

My Prosess

Persona

To find out users’ needs and goals I interviewed 5 potential users. As the next step, I created user personas, based on the information I had collected.

Wireframes

After creating mid-fidelity wireframes, I started testing the designs early on so that I could give the potential users some tasks to accomplish. I analyzed the users’ behaviors and decisions, and modified the wireframes based on the results of the analyses. Working on the visual section started afterwards.

Style Guide

Once the UX part of the design was done, I began to create mood boards and define the visual direction of the app. To ensure consistency across my design, I built this style guide. The style Guide includes typography, colors, iconography, UI elements, illustration, and imagery.

Preference Test

To find out which splash screens work better for my app, I conducted a preference test. Test results: – 37% of users voted for A, and 63% for B. – B performs better, but the difference is not statistically significant. It may perform better due to random chance alone. Therefore I sent the test to more users to get a significant result. Most of the users found the second screen to be better.
